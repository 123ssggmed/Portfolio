This firm is using AI productively 
across four departments. The exposure 
is not from the tools themselves — 
it is from the absence of basic rules 
around which data goes in, when human 
review applies, and what clients are 
told about how work is produced.

The five risks identified are operational 
and contractual, not regulatory per se. 
They are fixable in 30 days without 
outside counsel. The minimum controls 
pack in this sprint provides a 
ready-to-implement starting point.

RISK 1 — Client Data in AI Prompts
Impact: HIGH
Type: INFERENCE

Client briefs and contact information 
are likely included in prompts without 
a rule defining permitted data categories. 
This may conflict with confidentiality 
obligations in existing client agreements 
and create customer-notice exposure 
depending on data type.

Fix: Define permitted and restricted 
data categories in Guardrails v1.
Urgency: This week.

─────────────────────

RISK 2 — Contract Language Gap
Impact: HIGH
Type: INFERENCE

Client agreements likely predate current 
AI tool usage. If any active client has 
"original work" or "no AI" clauses, 
current delivery practices may conflict.

Fix: Review top 5 active client MSAs. 
Add AI-use disclosure clause to 
all new agreements going forward.
Urgency: This month.

─────────────────────

RISK 3 — No Human Review Standard
Impact: MEDIUM
Type: ASSUMPTION

Without a defined review rule, each 
employee decides independently when 
AI output needs checking before 
client delivery. Creates inconsistent 
quality and potential commercial 
claim exposure.

Fix: Define 3 output categories 
that always require human review.
Urgency: This month.

─────────────────────

RISK 4 — IP Ambiguity on Deliverables
Impact: MEDIUM
Type: INFERENCE

AI-generated creative work delivered 
as original output without disclosure 
creates ambiguity if a client disputes 
ownership or originality.

Fix: Standard language on deliverable 
cover notes and new contracts.
Urgency: Next contract cycle.

─────────────────────

RISK 5 — No Tool Approval Rule
Impact: MEDIUM
Type: FACT

No approval required before employees 
adopt new AI tools. Creates vendor 
data risk and inconsistent handling 
across the team.

Fix: Simple approval rule in 
Guardrails v1.
Urgency: This month.

Starter Internal AI Rules

   ChatGPT, Claude, Midjourney,
