# 📁 CASE STUDY 1 — Marketing Agency

---

## CLIENT PROFILE

| Campo | Detalle |
|---|---|
| **Industry** | Marketing & Creative Agency |
| **Location** | Austin, TX |
| **Size** | 23 employees |
| **Revenue** | $3.2M ARR |
| **AI Tools Used** | ChatGPT, Midjourney, HubSpot AI, Grammarly, Jasper |

---

## THE SITUATION

The agency was using AI tools across 4 departments (content, creative, strategy, and account management) with no formal policy, no vendor agreements reviewed, and client data being processed through third-party AI tools without disclosure in their contracts.

---

## RISKS IDENTIFIED

### 🔴 RISK 1 — Client Data in AI Prompts (HIGH)
**Impact:** Client briefs and contact information were likely included in prompts without a rule defining permitted data categories. This conflicts with confidentiality obligations in existing client agreements and creates customer-notice exposure.

**Financial Exposure:** Potential breach claims from 3 clients with explicit confidentiality clauses. Estimated exposure: **$45,000-$75,000** in contract penalties and reputational damage.

---

### 🔴 RISK 2 — Contract Language Gap (HIGH)
**Impact:** Client agreements predated current AI tool usage. 2 active clients had "original work" and "no AI" clauses in their MSAs — the agency was potentially in breach.

**Financial Exposure:** Contract termination risk on **$180,000 ARR** if clients discovered the practice.

---

### 🟡 RISK 3 — No Human Review Standard (MEDIUM)
**Impact:** Each employee decided independently when AI output needed checking before client delivery. Created inconsistent quality and potential commercial claim exposure.

**Financial Exposure:** Estimated **$12,000/year** in rework and client complaints.

---

### 🟡 RISK 4 — IP Ownership Ambiguity (MEDIUM)
**Impact:** AI-generated creative work delivered as original output without disclosure. Created ambiguity if a client disputes ownership or originality.

**Financial Exposure:** Potential IP dispute on **$35,000** in active projects.

---

### 🟢 RISK 5 — No Tool Approval Process (LOW)
**Impact:** No approval required before employees adopted new AI tools. Created vendor data risk and inconsistent handling across the team.

---

## OPPORTUNITIES IDENTIFIED

1. **AI Disclosure Addendum** → Turns risk into competitive advantage ("We use AI responsibly and transparently")

2. **AI-Native Service Tier** → Premium pricing for AI-augmented deliverables with full transparency. Potential **$40,000/year** additional revenue.

3. **Automate 3 Internal Workflows** → Recover 14 hours/week with zero exposure.

---

## DELIVERABLES PROVIDED

- [ ] 2-page Internal AI Policy (AI Use Guardrails v1)
- [ ] Client Contract AI Addendum Template
- [ ] Risk Matrix (5 risks ranked by urgency)
- [ ] 30-60-90 Day Implementation Roadmap

---

## THE RESULT

**Sprint delivered in 18 hours.**

Client implemented policy within 72 hours. Renegotiated 2 client contracts proactively. Avoided potential **$260,000** in exposure.

**Client testimonial:**
> "I didn't realize we were unknowingly in breach with 2 of our biggest clients. The sprint paid for itself in the first week."

---

## INVESTMENT: $500

# 📁 CASE STUDY 5 — Healthcare Practice

---

## CLIENT PROFILE

| Campo | Detalle |
|---|---|
| **Industry** | Private Medical Practice (Primary Care) |
| **Location** | Phoenix, AZ |
| **Size** | 6 physicians, 8 staff |
| **Revenue** | $2.8M ARR |
| **AI Tools Used** | ChatGPT (patient communication), Dragon Medical, IBM Watson, EHR AI features |

---

## THE SITUATION

The practice was using AI for appointment scheduling, patient communication, and clinical note transcription. However, HIPAA compliance had not been evaluated for any AI tools, and no Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) existed with AI vendors.

---

## RISKS IDENTIFIED

### 🔴 RISK 1 — HIPAA Violation (CRITICAL)
**Impact:** PHI (Protected Health Information) being processed through AI tools without BAAs. This is a direct HIPAA violation.

**Financial Exposure:** OCR enforcement. Civil penalties: **$100-$50,000 per violation** × potential thousands = **$1.5M+**. Criminal penalties possible.

---

### 🔴 RISK 2 — Clinical Decision Support Liability (HIGH)
**Impact:** AI used to suggest clinical decisions without proper documentation of physician review and judgment.

**Financial Exposure:** Medical malpractice + licensing issues. Exposure: **$500,000+**.

---

### 🔴 RISK 3 — Patient Consent Gap (HIGH)
**Impact:** No patient consent for AI-assisted communication or documentation.

**Financial Exposure:** Patient complaints + HIPAA violations. Exposure: **$50,000**.

---

### 🟡 RISK 4 — Vendor Security Review (MEDIUM)
**Impact:** No security assessment of AI vendors. Unknown data handling practices.

**Financial Exposure:** Breach liability. Exposure: **$200,000+**.

---

### 🟢 RISK 5 — Staff Training Gap (LOW)
**Impact:** No training on appropriate AI use with patient data.

---

## OPPORTUNITIES IDENTIFIED

1. **HIPAA-Safe AI Implementation** → Continue using AI without violation risk.

2. **Patient Trust Through Transparency** → Disclose AI use in patient intake. Differentiator.

3. **Efficiency Gains** → 8 hours/week recovered in documentation.

---

## DELIVERABLES PROVIDED

- [ ] AI Vendor HIPAA Compliance Checklist
- [ ] Business Associate Agreement Requirements for AI Vendors
- [ ] Patient Consent Form (AI-Assisted Care)
- [ ] Clinical AI Decision Documentation Protocol
- [ ] Staff AI Training Guidelines
- [ ] Risk Matrix (5 risks ranked)
- [ ] 30-60-90 Day HIPAA Compliance Plan

---

## THE RESULT

**Sprint delivered in 24 hours.**

Practice immediately discontinued use of 2 AI tools until BAAs were executed. Implemented patient consent form. Created vendor security review process.

**Avoided exposure:** Estimated **$500,000+** in potential HIPAA penalties and malpractice liability.

---

## INVESTMENT: $500
